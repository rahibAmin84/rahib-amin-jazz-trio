# Photo strategy for the personal brand site

You do **not** need a professional portrait, speaking photo, or contact portrait for the site to work well right now.

The site has been updated so those areas use designed text/visual panels instead of placeholder portraits. That means you can launch with real images you already have, then add polished portraits later when you are ready.

## Use now: priority images

### 1. Homepage hero

File:

```text
assets/images/hero-portrait.jpg
```

Best image:

- You playing guitar
- You in a creative environment
- A strong candid image with personality
- Does **not** need to look corporate

Purpose: show the full Rahib brand: technical, creative, human.

### 2. Music performance

File:

```text
assets/images/music-performance.jpg
```

Best image:

- You performing
- Guitar visible
- Strong energy or atmosphere

Purpose: lead the `/music-arts/` page.

### 3. Music trio / group

File:

```text
assets/images/music-trio.jpg
```

Best image:

- Trio/band photo
- Rehearsal or gig photo
- Stage or venue image

Purpose: show collaboration and performance context.

### 4. Music atmosphere

File:

```text
assets/images/music-atmosphere.jpg
```

Best image:

- Venue
- Guitar detail
- Amp/pedals
- Room/stage mood
- York/Richmond/city texture if meaningful

Purpose: support About, Music, and brand atmosphere.

### 5. About origin image

File:

```text
assets/images/about-origin.jpg
```

Best image:

- A meaningful place
- Travel/family-safe contextual image
- Instrument detail
- Notebook, records, street, city, or reflective image
- Does not need to be a portrait

Purpose: support the personal origin story without requiring a formal portrait.

## Not needed yet

These files can exist, but the pages no longer depend on them:

```text
assets/images/professional-portrait.jpg
assets/images/speaking-photo.jpg
assets/images/contact-portrait.jpg
```

Add them later only when you have genuinely strong images.

## Future photo wishlist

When convenient, get these images:

1. One warm professional portrait — not corporate headshot stiff; founder/operator feel.
2. One working photo — laptop, whiteboard, workshop, architecture diagram, or speaking candid.
3. One contact portrait — friendly, approachable, simple background.
4. One high-quality performance photo — guitar, expression, atmosphere.
5. One environmental image — York wall, jazz club, studio, or city texture.

## Rule of thumb

Use real, imperfect, emotionally credible photos over generic stock images.

A personal brand site should feel like a person with a point of view, not a template.
