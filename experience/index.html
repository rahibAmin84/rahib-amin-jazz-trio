WECX — Eckerd College Radio

General Manager · Student radio station rebuild

At Eckerd College, I became General Manager of WECX at a point when the radio station was not functioning. The work became an early lesson in systems ownership: diagnose the problem, find people with the right expertise, modernize the operating model, and create something others could actually use.

  • Tracked down technical help by physically going to RadioShack, asking for contacts, and finding an electrical engineer who could help bring the station back to life.
  • Helped rework a non-working radio console design that previously depended on hard-wired or soldered inputs directly into the board.
  • Worked with the electrician to create safer, more flexible input jacks so the station could connect record players, tape decks, CD players, 1/8-inch inputs, 1/4-inch inputs, and more modern audio sources.
  • Enabled the station to support broader programming and online streaming, turning a broken setup into a usable creative platform for students.
  • Left a short but meaningful legacy: within roughly a year and a half, the station became something friends and students could actually use, enjoy, and build around.

Professional Musician

Jazz Guitarist · Composer · Recording Artist · Creative Collaborator

Music has been one of the longest-running threads in my life. As a jazz guitarist, my voice draws from Hindustani music, Arabic melodies, avant-garde jazz, free improvisation, early American music, rock, soul, R&B, and bebop.

  • Earned Outstanding Solo Guitar Performance recognition at Berklee College of Music in Boston and McGill University in Montréal.
  • Performed at jazz festivals and composition settings across North America, developing a musical language shaped by Indian influence, modern jazz, and improvisation.
  • Released Suffice To Say with The BeSides in 2025, a locally formed Richmond jazz group.
  • Continue to perform in modern jazz trio settings and other projects with accomplished musicians crossing paths in Richmond, Virginia.
  • Bring the lessons of music — timing, structure, adaptation, listening, and collaboration — into my work in technology and leadership.
